# Product Classification Framework

## Purpose

This framework standardizes how Crown Labs products are categorized, described, valued, and presented across all documentation, investor materials, landing pages, and internal systems.

The objective is to maintain consistency, legal clarity, and operational transparency.

## Product Status Categories

### Active Product
A system currently functional, accessible, or operational.

Requirements:

- existing infrastructure
- active codebase
- deployable functionality
- validated architecture

### Prototype
A partially built system with validated concepts but incomplete implementation.

Requirements:

- defined architecture
- active development
- functional mockups or modules
- incomplete production readiness

### Concept System
A strategically defined product concept without active production implementation.

Requirements:

- documented feature set
- monetization strategy
- market positioning
- architectural planning

## Documentation Requirements

Every product dossier should contain:

- product summary
- mission statement
- problem solved
- feature breakdown
- architecture overview
- monetization strategy
- deployment status
- valuation rationale
- roadmap status

## Valuation Classification

Each product should clearly distinguish:

- current estimated value
- projected completed value
- liquidity potential
- licensing potential
- acquisition positioning

## Public Positioning Rules

Public facing materials must:

- avoid overstating completion
- distinguish prototypes from production systems
- communicate realistic capabilities
- avoid unverifiable technical claims

## Internal Portfolio Structure

Products are organized into categories:

### AI Systems
Products involving AI analysis, inference, automation, or augmentation.

### Security Systems
Products focused on monitoring, protection, surveillance, or emergency response.

### Psychology Systems
Products focused on behavioral insight, self improvement, assessment, or emotional intelligence.

### Communication Systems
Products focused on interaction, messaging, collaboration, or presence.

### Media Systems
Products focused on content generation, branding, editing, or publishing.

## Brand Alignment Rules

Every product must:

- visually align with Crown Labs standards
- maintain premium presentation quality
- preserve ecosystem consistency
- support future modular expansion
