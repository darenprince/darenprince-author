Financial & Growth Projections (Investor View)

Scope: Portfolio-level projections based on current asset status · Methodology: Conservative, Base, and Upside scenarios · Currency: USD · Disclaimer: Illustrative projections, not guarantees

Portfolio composition (relevant to financials)

Revenue-generating categories:

  • Consumer SaaS (subscriptions)
  • Prosumer / creator tools
  • Safety & privacy platforms
  • Professional / enterprise licensing
  • IP (books, frameworks, licensing)

Risk mitigation:

  • No single-product dependency
  • Multiple launch timelines
  • Mixed price points and buyer types
  • Combination of recurring and transactional revenue

Current asset stage distribution

  • App Store ready: 2
  • Beta / late prototype: 4
  • Prototype / defined systems: 4
  • Published IP: 1
  • Narrative / framework IP: 4

This supports staggered revenue activation rather than a single launch moment.

  • No breakout product

Upside assumptions

  • Strong brand leverage across portfolio

Capital efficiency

  • Initial capital requirements are low relative to asset base
  • Primary spending unlocks existing value (licensing, launch, distribution)
  • Portfolio design reduces binary failure risk
  • Cash flow reinvestment reduces dependency on follow-on capital

Exit pathways (non-dependent)

  • Individual product acquisition
  • Strategic licensing deals
  • Spin-out of enterprise systems
  • IP sale or royalty structures
  • Portfolio-level acquisition

Investor takeaway

  • Assets already exist
  • Downside risk is capped by diversification
  • Upside scales non-linearly with adoption
  • Capital accelerates value realization, not discovery
  • Portfolio behaves more like a mini-holding company than a single startup

Ethics & Limits

Hard boundaries that keep the work honest.

Privacy-first defaults

Products that handle sensitive inputs default to minimal retention, clear consent, and transparent control.

Decision-support only

No product positions itself as medical, legal, or therapy advice. Outputs are support signals, not directives.

No retention by design

Where applicable, products avoid storing data. If retention is required, it is explicit, scoped, and documented.
